Interpreting Refractory Supraventricular Tachycardia in Pediatric Patients

Reviewing a case from today's rotation: 8-year-old male presenting with SVT unresponsive to initial vagal maneuvers and first dose of Adenosine (0.1 mg/kg). The ECG shows characteristic narrow QRS complex but with unusual P-wave morphology in lead II.

Is it possible we are looking at an orthodromic AVRT with a retrograde conduction path that is mimicking a more standard AVNRT? Seeking input from those currently on the Peds rotation or Cardiology faculty.

"Great observation, Aris. If you look closely at the ST-segment in lead V1, you can see a pseudo-r' wave which is highly suggestive of typical slow-fast AVNRT. However, given the resistance to the first Adenosine dose, we should consider if the pathway has a longer refractory period. Did you observe any retrograde P-waves after the termination?"
