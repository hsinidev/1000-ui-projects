The Architecture of Inspiration: A Semester in Rome

Elena Rossi

Class of 2024 • Architecture & Design

Walking through the streets of Rome is like leafing through the largest pop-up history book ever made. Every corner turned reveals a new layer of the past—from the grit of the Roman Republic to the soaring ambition of the Baroque era. My semester here wasn't just about credits; it was about seeing the blueprints of civilization in three dimensions.

Morning Light on the Pantheon

My daily commute involved crossing the Piazza della Rotonda. While most tourists saw a monument, I saw a masterclass in structural engineering. The way the light hits the oculus at 10:00 AM creates a tangible column of air that makes you realize how architecture can manipulate not just stone, but the very atmosphere itself.

"Rome didn't just teach me how to build; it taught me why we build. It’s for the legacy of the light we leave behind."

During my four months, GlobalStudy arranged exclusive access to the restricted archives of the Vatican Museums. Being able to touch the original sketches of Bernini while a soft Italian breeze wafted through the high windows is a memory that will forever influence my professional practice.
