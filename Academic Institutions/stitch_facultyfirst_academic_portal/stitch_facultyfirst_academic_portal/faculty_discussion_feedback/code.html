Revised Tenure Review Timeline feedback needed

Dr. Margaret Sullivan • Associate Dean of Academic Affairs

The proposed revision aims to extend the pre-tenure period by one academic year for all tenure-track faculty joining after August 2024. This change is intended to provide more robust research and publication cycles, particularly in fields with longer review windows.

We are seeking specific feedback on how this might impact multi-disciplinary research initiatives and whether departmental sub-committees should have the power to opt-out of the extension. Please provide your insights below by EOD Friday.

+109
Published Oct 24, 2023 · 34 Comments
AK
Dr. Alistair Khan 3 hours ago

While the extension is welcome for high-impact publishing, we must ensure it doesn't inadvertently stall laboratory grant cycles which rely on tenure-track stability for long-term doctoral student funding.

EL
Prof. Elena Lopez 2 hours ago

Dr. Khan makes a vital point. The bridge between doctoral mentorship and the tenure clock is fragile. Could we build in a 'mid-cycle review' that secures the mentorship status even with the extended clock?

JW
Dr. James Whittaker 5 hours ago

I support the departmental sub-committee opt-out clause. Specifically, the Humanities department has expressed that the current 6-year window is already sufficient for our publication cycle.
