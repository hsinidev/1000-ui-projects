Synopsis

"The Structure of Silence" is an exhaustive visual study of the spatial relationships within Post-War socialist housing complexes. Voss-Müller avoids traditional narration, opting instead for a meticulously recorded soundscape of the buildings themselves. The film serves as both a cinematic document of architectural intent and a haunting exploration of the human experience within idealized urban structures. This restored edition includes previously suppressed sequences from the 1975 Berlin exhibition.

Curator's Note

"Voss-Müller doesn't just film concrete; she films the air that lives between it. This work remains the definitive text on the sonic ecology of the Soviet-era apartment block."

— Dr. Julian Thorne, Lead Curator
