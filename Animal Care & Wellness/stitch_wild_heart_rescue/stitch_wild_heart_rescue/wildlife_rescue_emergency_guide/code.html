URGENT GUIDANCE

What to do if you find an animal

Your quick and calm actions can save a life. Follow these expert-verified steps to ensure the safety of both the animal and yourself.

Emergency Hotline

Available 24/7 for immediate rescue dispatch

STEP 01

Observe from a Distance

Not every animal that looks "lost" needs rescuing. Many young animals are left alone while parents forage. Watch for signs of actual distress: bleeding, shivering, or visible wounds.

Specific Species Protocols

nest_multi_room

Birds

If you find a fledgling (has feathers), leave it be. Parents are likely nearby. If it's a nestling (pink skin), try to find the nest and gently put it back.

QUICK TIP

It's a myth that parents will reject babies if touched by humans.

pets

Mammals

Use thick gloves. For squirrels or rabbits, place in a small box with a soft towel. Keep in a warm, dark, and quiet place away from children and pets.

SAFETY FIRST

Never attempt to handle adult raccoons, foxes, or bats without professional gear.

settings_ethernet

Reptiles

Turtles crossing the road should be moved in the direction they are headed. Do not relocate them to a "better" pond; they have small home ranges.

PRO ADVICE

Always wash your hands thoroughly after handling any reptile or amphibian.

Prepare for Transport

If directed by our dispatchers, secure the animal in a ventilated box. Do not offer food or water, as this can often be fatal to an animal in shock.

  • 1 Line a box with a soft cloth
  • 2 Keep the box closed and secure
  • 3 Minimize all noise and handling
