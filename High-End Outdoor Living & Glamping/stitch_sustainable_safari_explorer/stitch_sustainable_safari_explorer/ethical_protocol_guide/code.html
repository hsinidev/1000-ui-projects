Ethical Protocol Guide

As stewards of the wild, our presence must be a ghost in the landscape. This ledger outlines the sacred boundaries of the expedition, ensuring the longevity of the ecosystems we traverse.

Reference: 2024-V1

SEC-001 / WILDLIFE

Wildlife Distances

100m nature

Predator Buffer

Lions, Leopards, and Hyenas. Maintain absolute silence. No sudden movements or direct eye contact if outside the vehicle.

50m eco

Herbivore Safety

Elephants, Rhinos, and Buffalo. Give right of way at all times. Observe ear position and vocalizations for stress signs.

25m flutter_dash

Avian & Small Fauna

Birds and reptiles. Do not flush nesting birds or block access to watering holes. Use optical zoom exclusively.

Leave No Trace

  • Pack out every trace of presence, including organic peels and waste.
  • Stick to established game tracks to prevent soil compaction and erosion.
  • Use biodegradable hygiene products exclusively at base camps.

Cultural Etiquette

01

Photography Consent

Always ask for permission before photographing people or dwellings. Share the image result as a gesture of transparency.

02

Trade & Bartering

Respect local economies. Avoid aggressive bartering and prioritize purchases from community-owned cooperatives.

03

Traditional Knowledge

Listen more than you speak. Local guides are repositories of centuries-old ecological wisdom.

"We are visitors, not owners."

— Expedition Manifesto, p. 14

The Silent Observer

The ultimate ethical protocol is the practice of 'The Silent Observer.' This involves tuning your senses to the frequency of the wild, minimizing your acoustic and olfactory footprint, and departing before your presence alters any natural behavior.
