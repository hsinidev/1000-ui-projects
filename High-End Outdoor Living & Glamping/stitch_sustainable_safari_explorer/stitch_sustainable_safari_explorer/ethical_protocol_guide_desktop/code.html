The Ethical Protocol Guide

This digital ledger serves as your primary reference for responsible engagement with the fragile ecosystems of the African savannah. We advocate for a "Quiet Footprint" philosophy, ensuring that our presence remains a silent tribute to the wild.

Wildlife Distances

Section 01 / 03
distance Buffer Zone Logic

Minimum 30 Meters Standard

Approaching wildlife too closely disrupts natural behavior, hunting patterns, and mating rituals. Our protocols prioritize animal welfare over visual proximity.

Large Cats 25m
Pachyderms 40m
Rhino 50m+

Observation HUD

  • Engine Status OFF
  • Noise Level < 20dB
  • Max Capacity 2 Vehicles
warning

Warning Signs

Flapping ears (elephants), flattened ears (cats), or direct staring indicate stress. Retreat immediately.

Section 02 — Cultural Heritage

Engagement with Local Communities

Human heritage is inextricably linked to the land. When visiting indigenous communities, remember that you are an invited guest into a living history. Respect for privacy and cultural sovereignty is paramount.

01

Photography Consent

Always seek verbal permission before capturing images of individuals or domestic spaces.

02

Economic Integrity

Support local artisans directly. Avoid aggressive bartering; pay fair prices that respect the craftsmanship.

03

Dress Code

Adhere to modest attire guidelines as specified by village elders in the pre-arrival briefing.

04

Language

Learning basic greetings in the local dialect is not just useful, it is a sign of profound respect.

Leave No Trace

1

Waste Management

Everything brought into the bush must be brought out. This includes organic waste (peels, seeds) which can disrupt the diet of local micro-fauna.

2

Sound & Light Pollution

Use red-light filters after dusk to prevent disorientation of nocturnal species. Maintain conversational tones at all times.

3

Physical Integrity

Do not remove stones, feathers, or flora. These are integral components of the nutrient cycle and habitat structure.
