A diamond's beauty is defined by four universal characteristics. Discover how precision craftsmanship meets nature's finest art.

diamond 01

Cut

Not to be confused with shape, the cut refers to how well a diamond's facets interact with light. A perfect cut unleashes the fire and brilliance within.

palette 02

Color

In white diamonds, the absence of color is the measure of value. Ranging from D (colorless) to Z (light yellow), the most prized stones are as clear as pure water.

visibility 03

Clarity

Diamonds are formed under immense pressure, often creating internal 'inclusions'. Clarity measures the purity of the stone under 10x magnification.

Carat

Carat measures the diamond's weight, not size. While larger stones are rare, the true value of a carat is unlocked through its harmony with Cut, Color, and Clarity.
