Care &
Preservation

Maintaining the integrity of archival garments requires a commitment to slow, intentional preservation techniques. This guide outlines the essential protocols for protecting your collection.

Serial No. CP-2024-001

01

Storage Protocols

inventory_2
  • Atmospheric Control

    Store in a cool, dry place away from direct UV exposure. Optimal temperature should remain between 18°C and 22°C.

  • Structural Support

    Heavy knits must be folded flat to prevent vertical stretching. Use padded hangers for structured silk and wool outerwear.

  • Acid-Free Tissue

    Interleave delicate fabrics with unbuffered acid-free tissue paper to prevent dye migration and creasing.

02

Cleaning Standards

cleaning_services

The Golden Rule

"Cleaning should only be performed when absolutely necessary. Every intervention carries a risk of degradation."

Localized Treatment

Use distilled water and a neutral pH detergent for spot cleaning. Avoid vigorous rubbing.

Specialist Consultation

For silk, leather, and heavily embellished pieces, consult a textile conservator rather than a standard dry cleaner.

03

Manual Handling

pan_tool

Archival pieces should be treated as museum artifacts. Skin oils and salts can cause irreversible yellowing over decades.
