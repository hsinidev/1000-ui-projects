Frequently Questioned Principles

What is the optimal RT60 for a professional recording studio? expand_more
The optimal RT60 (Reverberation Time to -60dB) for a studio typically falls between 0.3s and 0.5s for control rooms to ensure accurate monitoring. Recording rooms vary widely depending on the desired aesthetic, from "dead" rooms (0.2s) to vibrant orchestral spaces (1.5s+).
How do I choose between a porous absorber and a tuned trap? expand_more
Porous absorbers (like high-density mineral wool) are broadband and effective at higher frequencies. Tuned traps (Helmholtz resonators or limp-mass membranes) are engineered to target specific low-frequency modal issues below 100Hz without over-deadening the high end of the room.
What is the difference between Soundproofing and Acoustic Treatment? expand_more
Soundproofing (Isolation) focuses on keeping sound from entering or leaving a space by using mass and decoupling. Acoustic Treatment (Conditioning) focuses on the quality of sound *within* the space by controlling reflections, resonance, and echo.
