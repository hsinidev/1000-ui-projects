Protocols, Safety & Logistics

Is Cryotherapy safe for clinical use?

expand_more
Our whole-body cryotherapy chambers use liquid nitrogen-cooled air in a strictly monitored environment. Every session is overseen by a certified Frost Technician and adheres to ISO medical safety standards.

What is the recommended protocol duration?

expand_more
Standard Arctic sessions last between 2.5 to 3 minutes at temperatures reaching -140°C. For optimal anti-inflammatory response, we recommend 2-3 sessions per week during intensive training cycles.

Studio conduct & clothing requirements

expand_more
Protective gear (gloves, socks, slippers, and face masks) is provided. Clients are required to wear dry cotton undergarments or swimwear. No jewelry or metallic items are permitted inside the chamber.

Digital Biomarker Tracking integration

expand_more
Our system integrates with WHOOP, Oura, and Garmin to correlate session data with your heart rate variability (HRV) and sleep scores, providing a holistic view of your thermal recovery impact.
