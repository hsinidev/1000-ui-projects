THE ARCHITECTURE OF PRECISION

CRISPR-Cas9 is a specialized system of DNA sequences found in the genomes of prokaryotic organisms. In genetic engineering, it functions as a highly programmable pair of "molecular scissors," allowing researchers to locate, isolate, and modify specific segments of genomic data with architectural precision.

PROBE_01 Targeting sequence identification via gRNA alignment.
PROBE_02 Cas9 endonuclease activation and double-strand break execution.

01 // THE gRNA CARRIER

The guide RNA (gRNA) is a synthetic piece of RNA that combines the functions of the crRNA and the tracrRNA. It is engineered to have a base sequence that is complementary to the target DNA sequence. This is the search protocol of the CRISPR operation.

02 // CAS9 ENDONUCLEASE

Once the gRNA identifies the target sequence, the Cas9 protein (the endonuclease) performs a physical intervention. It cuts the DNA double helix at a specific location, creating what is known as a double-strand break (DSB).
