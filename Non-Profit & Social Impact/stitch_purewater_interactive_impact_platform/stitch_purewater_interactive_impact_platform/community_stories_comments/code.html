Engineering Hope: The Well Building Process

Documented by Field Director Sarah Jenks

The journey to clean water in Chididi wasn't just about drilling a hole; it was a collaborative engineering effort that combined modern technology with local geological wisdom. Our team spent three weeks conducting hydrogeological surveys to locate the most sustainable aquifer.

Phase 1: Deep Aquifer Access

Traditional shallow wells often fail during the dry season. For Amara's community, we utilized a high-pressure pneumatic rig to reach a depth of 85 meters, tapping into a fracture-zone aquifer that provides a constant flow of 30 liters per minute, even in the harshest drought conditions.

Phase 2: Solar Integration

Sustainability is at the heart of our 'Life in Motion' philosophy. We installed a 400W monocrystalline solar array that powers a submersible brushless pump. This eliminates the need for expensive diesel fuel and ensures the system operates autonomously for the next 20 years.
