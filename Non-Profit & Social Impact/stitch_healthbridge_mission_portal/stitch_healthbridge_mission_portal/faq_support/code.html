Volunteer Info

What are the requirements for medical volunteers?

expand_more
Licensed medical professionals (MD, RN, PA, etc.) must provide current board certification and a valid passport. Non-medical volunteers must pass a background check and complete a mandatory 12-hour disaster response orientation.

How long do typical mission deployments last?

expand_more
Standard missions last between 14 and 21 days. This includes 2 days of on-site logistical training and 1 day of debriefing before return travel. Emergency rapid-response deployments may vary from 7 to 30 days.

Medical Protocols

How are patient records managed in remote areas?

expand_more
We utilize a proprietary offline-first electronic health record (EHR) system. Data is stored locally on encrypted tablets and automatically syncs to our secure central servers whenever a satellite or cellular connection becomes available.

What pharmaceutical standards does HealthBridge follow?

expand_more
All medications are sourced from WHO-prequalified suppliers. We strictly adhere to the Interagency Emergency Health Kit (IEHK) standards for cold-chain management and pharmaceutical inventory control.
payments

Donations

Where does my financial donation go?

expand_more
Approximately 88% of every dollar donated goes directly to mission logistics, medical supplies, and local healthcare worker support. The remaining 12% is used for global administration and critical fundraising efforts.

Do you accept physical medical equipment donations?

expand_more
Yes, we accept new or professionally refurbished medical equipment that meets our current mission needs. Please contact our logistics team at donations@healthbridge.org for a list of approved items and shipping instructions.

Still have questions?

Our support team is available 24/7 to assist with urgent medical logistics and donor inquiries. Reach out through any of our clinical communication channels.
