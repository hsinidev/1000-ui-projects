The Journey

Growing up in a community where medical resources were scarce, I witnessed firsthand how engineering solutions could bridge the gap in healthcare accessibility. My dream of becoming a Bioengineer isn't just about a degree; it's about developing low-cost diagnostic tools for underserved rural clinics.

Currently maintaining a 3.9 GPA at Johns Hopkins, I am specializing in tissue engineering and biomechanics. Every contribution to my ScholarFund doesn't just pay for credits—it invests in a future where healthcare technology is a right, not a privilege. I have already completed two internships at leading research labs, focusing on synthetic organ development.
