LASIK vs PRK:
The Path to Clarity

Understanding which corrective procedure aligns with your unique corneal profile and lifestyle. Science-backed precision for life-changing results.

Technical Deep Dive

Comparing the two gold standards of refractive surgery. Both procedures use an excimer laser to reshape the cornea, but the method of access differs.

layers

LASIK

Laser-Assisted In Situ Keratomileusis. A procedure involving the creation of a thin protective flap to access the underlying corneal tissue.

  • speed Rapid recovery (24-48 hours)
  • visibility Immediate visual improvement
  • check_circle Minimal post-op discomfort
blur_on

PRK

Photorefractive Keratectomy. The outer layer of the cornea (epithelium) is gently removed rather than creating a flap.

  • health_and_safety Ideal for thin corneas
  • sports_martial_arts Better for contact sports
  • verified_user No risk of flap complications
