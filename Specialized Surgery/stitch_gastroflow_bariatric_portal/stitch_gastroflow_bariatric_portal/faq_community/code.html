Frequently Asked Questions

What are the main differences between Gastric Bypass and Sleeve?

expand_less
Both procedures are effective for weight loss but work differently. Gastric Sleeve involves removing approximately 80% of the stomach, leaving a banana-shaped tube. Gastric Bypass (Roux-en-Y) creates a small pouch from the stomach and connects it directly to the small intestine. Bypass has a metabolic component that often leads to faster resolution of Type 2 diabetes.
