Common Inquiries

What defines a 'Certified Archival' record?

expand_more
A 'Certified Archival' record represents the pinnacle of documentation. It requires three generations of molecular verification, verified photographic evidence, and a complete health screening dossier. These records are permanent fixtures in The Custodian’s main vaults.

How are historical pedigrees reconstructed?

expand_more
Our historians utilize physical ledgers, period photography, and advanced genetic tracing to bridge gaps in historical documentation. This process often takes 3-6 months and involves cross-referencing with global canine archives.

Are digital certificates legally binding?

expand_more
Yes. Every digital certificate issued by The Custodian is backed by a secure cryptographic signature. However, for elite breeders, we recommend requesting a Physical Wax-Sealed Certificate for archival preservation.

Protocol for amending archival data?

expand_more
Amending a record requires formal petition to the Registry Council. You must provide notarized evidence of the discrepancy and, if necessary, secondary genetic verification from an approved laboratory.

Cannot find your answer?

Direct your inquiry to our archival staff. Please include the Registry ID of the animal in question for expedited service.
