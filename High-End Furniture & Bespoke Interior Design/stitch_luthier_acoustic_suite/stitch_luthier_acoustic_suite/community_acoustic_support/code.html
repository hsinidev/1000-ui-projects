Engineering FAQ

Standing waves (modes) are calculated using the room dimensions. For a mahogany-clad space, the absorption coefficient of the wood (typically 0.10 - 0.15 at mid-frequencies) must be factored into the Sabine equation.

f = c / (2L) * n
[c = speed of sound, L = dimension, n = mode order]

To prevent mechanical vibration transfer, use high-density neoprene pads or spring-loaded isolators between the bench legs and the floor. This ensures the instrument being worked on doesn't pick up structural noise.

Precision engineering requires a constant 45-50% RH (Relative Humidity). Fluctuations beyond 5% can alter the wood's density and significantly shift the harmonic peak of a soundboard.
