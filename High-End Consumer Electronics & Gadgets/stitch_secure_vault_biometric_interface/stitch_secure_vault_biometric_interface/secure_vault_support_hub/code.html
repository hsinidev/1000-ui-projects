Core Inquiries

01 How is the hardware-level entropy generated?
expand_more
The Secure-Vault uses a dual-source True Random Number Generator (TRNG) utilizing quantum tunneling noise as its primary entropy source. This is further hardened through an EAL7+ certified secure element to ensure absolute cryptographic uniqueness for every seed generated.
02 Procedure for EAL7+ recovery after hardware failure?
expand_more
In the event of hardware failure, utilize your 24-word recovery seed on a new Secure-Vault unit. The 'Cold Handshake' protocol will re-establish your identity without ever exposing keys to a networked environment.
03 Managing multi-signature 'Air-Gap' handshakes?
expand_more

Multi-sig operations require QR-based data transmission to maintain the air-gap security barrier. Follow these steps:

  • Initialize Partially Signed Transaction (PSBT)
  • Scan QR code with air-gapped device
  • Authorize with biometric physical verification
  • Broadcast final signed blob
04 Operational limits for extreme temperature storage?
expand_more
The V-SERIES 4.2 chassis is rated for storage between -40°C and +85°C. Operational performance is guaranteed between -10°C and +55°C. Beyond these limits, thermal shutdown protocols will engage to protect the secure element.
