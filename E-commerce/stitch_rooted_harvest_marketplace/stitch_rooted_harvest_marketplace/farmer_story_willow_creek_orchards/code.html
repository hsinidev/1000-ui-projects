Willow Creek Orchards

Led by the Miller family since 1924, harvesting heritage apples and stone fruits with a commitment to regenerative soil health.

A Century of Stewardship

Nestled in the fertile valley of the Blue Mountains, Willow Creek Orchards isn't just a place of production—it's a living legacy. Founded by Elias Miller over a hundred years ago, the farm has survived through droughts and economic shifts by staying true to one simple principle: the land gives back what you put in.

Today, Thomas and Sarah Miller manage over 40 varieties of heritage fruits, some of which were thought to be lost to history. They've eschewed industrial chemical farming in favor of multi-species cover cropping and natural pest management, ensuring that every bite of fruit is as clean as the mountain air that surrounds it.

"We don't see ourselves as owners of this land, but as its temporary guardians. Our job is to leave this soil richer than we found it for the next three generations." — Thomas Miller
