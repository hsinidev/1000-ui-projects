Periodontal health is the foundation of a lasting smile. We utilize conservative, tissue-sparing techniques to treat gum disease and restore your mouth to its natural, healthy state.

Gum Disease Treatments

Gum disease is more than just a dental issue; it's a systemic health concern. Our approach focuses on thorough cleaning, ongoing maintenance, and when necessary, precise surgical intervention to save your natural teeth.

cleaning_services

Scaling & Root Planing

Often referred to as a "deep cleaning," this non-surgical procedure is the first line of defense against gum disease. We meticulously remove plaque and tartar from above and below the gum line.

  • check_circle Removes deep bacterial deposits
  • check_circle Smooths root surfaces to prevent re-attachment
  • check_circle Promotes gum tissue re-attachment

Periodontal Maintenance

Regular visits are vital for patients who have been treated for periodontal disease. Unlike a standard cleaning, maintenance focuses on monitoring the health of the bone and soft tissues.

Recommended Interval

Every 3-4 Months

Osseous Surgery

For advanced gum disease that hasn't responded to non-surgical treatments, Osseous surgery allows us to reduce pocket depths and reshape damaged bone to create a more maintainable environment.

Pocket Reduction

Reducing the space where bacteria hide to stop the progression of disease.

Bone Reshaping

Correcting defects caused by infection to restore structural integrity.
