Post-Operative Recovery Portal

Clinical guidance for the first 72 hours following your surgical procedure. Follow these instructions precisely to ensure optimal healing and minimize complications.

Immediate Care

The First 24 Hours

Your body is in the acute healing phase. Rest is mandatory. Keep your head elevated at a 45-degree angle even while sleeping to reduce swelling.

ice_skating

Ice Therapy

20 mins on / 20 mins off

no_drinks

No Straws

Prevents dry socket

Professional Support

  • Bite Firmly: Keep the initial gauze in place for at least 45 minutes without shifting.
  • No Spitting: Avoid creating suction in the mouth; wipe away excess fluid gently.
  • Liquid Diet: Only cold liquids or soft room-temperature foods (yogurt, pudding).

Prescribed Regimen

Begin taking your prescribed pain medication before the local anesthetic wears off. Do not take medication on an empty stomach to avoid nausea.

Antibiotics

If prescribed antibiotics, ensure you complete the entire course even if you feel no signs of infection.

Tea Bag Method

"If gauze fails, use a moistened tea bag (black tea) wrapped in gauze. The tannic acid in the tea bag helps to form a clot by constricting blood vessels."

Healing Milestones

TIMELINE WHAT TO EXPECT ACTION
24-48 Hours Maximum swelling and stiffness. Continue Ice; Start salt water rinses.
72 Hours Swelling begins to subside. Switch from cold to warm compresses.
7-10 Days Stitches may dissolve or be removed. Resume normal brushing carefully.
