Patient Sedation & Anesthesia

We provide tailored anesthesia solutions to ensure absolute comfort and safety during surgical procedures. Our facility maintains hospital-grade monitoring standards for every case.

Levels of Sedation

Selected based on procedure complexity and patient preference.

vital_signs Deep Sedation

Intravenous (IV) Sedation

The most common choice for surgical procedures. Medications are administered directly into the bloodstream, providing immediate relaxation and amnesia of the procedure.

  • Rapid onset of effect
  • Precise dosage control
  • High level of patient comfort
medical_mask Conscious Sedation

Oral Conscious Sedation

A prescribed sedative taken prior to the appointment. While the patient remains conscious and able to respond, they experience a profound sense of calm and reduced anxiety.

  • No needles required
  • Gradual, gentle relaxation
  • Effective for mild anxiety
healing Localized

Local Anesthesia

Used in conjunction with all other forms of sedation to ensure the surgical site is completely numb. For minor procedures, this may be used as a standalone option.

  • Complete site numbness
  • Used for all surgeries
  • Quick recovery time

Surgical Safety Protocol

Our clinical team follows a rigorous 4-stage safety monitoring process for every patient under sedation.

Certifications

BOARD CERTIFIED SURGEONS

ACLS/BLS CERTIFIED NURSING STAFF

STATE LICENSED SEDATION FACILITY

Step 01

Pre-Anesthetic Evaluation

Comprehensive review of medical history, current medications, and cardiac health to determine the safest sedation profile.

Step 02

Continuous Monitoring

Real-time tracking of blood pressure, heart rate, oxygen saturation, and EKG throughout the entire procedure duration.

Step 03

Controlled Emergence

Patients are gradually brought out of sedation in our private recovery suite under direct supervision of clinical staff.

Step 04

Post-Op Release

Clinical discharge criteria must be met before release to the pre-arranged transportation provider.
