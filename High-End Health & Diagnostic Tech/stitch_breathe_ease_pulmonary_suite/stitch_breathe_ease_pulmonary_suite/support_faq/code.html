Frequently Asked Questions

medical_information How often should I replace the spirometer bacterial filters?
expand_more

To maintain high precision and ensure patient safety, bacterial filters must be replaced after every patient session. For personal home units, filters should be replaced every 30 days or if you notice any moisture buildup. Always use Breathe-Ease certified high-flow filters to maintain sensor calibration integrity.

info

PRO TIP: Stock up on filters in the "Diagnostics Shop" tab to avoid testing delays.

bluetooth_searching My inhaler tracker isn't syncing with the dashboard.
expand_more

First, ensure that Bluetooth is enabled on your tablet or smartphone. If the device remains disconnected, perform a hard reset by holding the sync button on the inhaler cap for 10 seconds until the light pulses cyan. Ensure the Breathe-Ease app has permission to access nearby devices in your OS settings.

query_stats What do the 'Yellow Zone' alerts in my spirometry logs mean?
expand_more

The Yellow Zone typically indicates that your FEV1 (Forced Expiratory Volume) is between 50% and 80% of your predicted personal best. This suggests a potential narrowing of the airways. You should consult your customized Asthma Action Plan and consider contacting your healthcare provider if levels do not return to the Green Zone after using rescue medication.

cloud_sync Can I export my diagnostic data for a second opinion?
expand_more

Yes. Navigate to the 'Patient Management' section, select the desired date range, and click the 'Export HIPAA-Secure Report' button. You can generate a comprehensive PDF or a raw data CSV file to share with other medical professionals securely.
