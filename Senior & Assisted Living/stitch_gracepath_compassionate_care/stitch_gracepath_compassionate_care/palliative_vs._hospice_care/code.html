Understanding Your Options

Palliative vs. Hospice Care

Choosing the right path for a loved one is a journey of love and dignity. While both care types focus on comfort and quality of life, they serve different needs at different times.

spa

Palliative Care

Specialized medical care for people living with a serious illness. This type of care is focused on providing relief from the symptoms and stress of the illness.

check_circle Can begin at any stage of illness
check_circle Provided alongside curative treatment
check_circle Focused on quality of life & symptom relief
favorite

Hospice Care

Compassionate care for people in the final phase of an incurable illness so that they may live as fully and comfortably as possible.

check_circle Begins when curative treatment stops
check_circle Focuses exclusively on comfort (palliation)
check_circle Requires a prognosis of 6 months or less

Detailed Comparison

Attribute Palliative Care Hospice Care
Goals of Care Improve comfort while pursuing active treatments or cures. Focus solely on pain management and emotional support.
Eligibility Anyone with a serious illness, regardless of life expectancy. Patients with a terminal diagnosis (6 months or less).
Where Provided Hospitals, clinics, and occasionally at home. Wherever the patient calls "home" (Residence, SNF, Hospital).
Care Team Specialized doctors, nurses, and social workers. Interdisciplinary team including spiritual and volunteer support.

A Shared Heart of Care

Regardless of the choice, both paths share a fundamental core: dignity, respect, and a commitment to ensuring that no one walks through serious illness alone.

"The focus isn't on the illness—it's on the person living with it."
