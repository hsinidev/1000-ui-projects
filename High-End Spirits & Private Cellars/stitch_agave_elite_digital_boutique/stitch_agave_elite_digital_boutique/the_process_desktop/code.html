The Art of Distillation

SEED TO BOTTLE: A JOURNEY OF PATIENCE

Growth Chronology

01

PLANTING

Blue Weber Agave sprouts are hand-selected and planted in iron-rich volcanic soil.

For seven to nine years, the plant absorbs the elements, concentrating complex sugars in its core.

02

MATURATION

03

THE JIMA

Expert Jimadors harvest the heart (piña) using the Coa de Jima with surgical precision.

The extracted nectar begins its transformation through roasting and fermentation.

04

DISTILLATION

Stage One

THE TERROIR OF LOS ALTOS

Our agave is grown exclusively in the red, iron-rich volcanic soils of Los Altos de Jalisco. The high altitude and extreme temperature fluctuations between day and night stress the agave, forcing it to produce the unique floral and sweet notes that define Agave Elite.

Soil Composition

"The earth gives what the sky demands. Our minerals are the backbone of every drop."

Stage Two

THE ALCHEMY OF COPPER

We utilize traditional copper pot stills, meticulously maintained and polished. This architectural approach to distillation ensures the removal of impurities while preserving the delicate esters and fatty acids that provide our tequila's velvety mouthfeel and complex aroma.

STILL TYPE Hand-Hammered Copper
PURITY LEVEL 99.9% Heart Cut
Stage Three

CHRONICLE OF THE CASK

Our maturation process is a dialogue between wood and spirit. Using a combination of American White Oak and French Oak barrels, we allow the tequila to rest in temperature-controlled caves. Here, it breathes through the charred staves, acquiring notes of vanilla, toasted almond, and subtle smoke.
