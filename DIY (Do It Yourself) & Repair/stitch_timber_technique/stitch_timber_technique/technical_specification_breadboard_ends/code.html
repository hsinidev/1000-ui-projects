Technical Specification: Breadboard Ends

The quintessential joinery solution for managing the dynamic nature of solid wood table tops.

01

Structural Purpose

Breadboard ends are narrow strips of wood joined to the ends of a larger panel. Their primary function is two-fold: aesthetic concealment of end grain and mechanical restraint against warping.

"The joint must act as a straightedge while remaining completely independent of the panel's width-wise movement."
  • check_circle Prevents cupping by providing lateral rigidity.
  • check_circle Allows for seasonal expansion and contraction across the grain.
  • check_circle Protects vulnerable end-grain from moisture absorption.

Internal Anatomy

The central tenon is glued and pinned, while outer tenons remain free of glue with elongated slots for movement.

Construction Detail

Success lies in the precision of the tongue-and-groove interface. The tongue should be roughly 1/3 of the overall board thickness.

Drawbore Pins

Holes in tenons are slightly offset toward the shoulder to pull the joint tight upon insertion of the peg.

Elongated Holes

The outer holes in the tenons must be oval-shaped to allow the pins to slide as the panel expands.

Material Considerations

Choice of species dictates the movement ratio. High-movement species require more aggressive tolerances.

Hardwoods

Oak, Hickory, Walnut. High stability, but significant seasonal movement in flat-sawn cuts.

Softwoods

Pine, Cedar, Fir. Generally more movement; require deeper mortises to prevent gaps.
