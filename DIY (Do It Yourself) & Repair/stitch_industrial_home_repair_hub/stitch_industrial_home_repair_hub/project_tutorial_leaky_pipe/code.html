Master the fundamental skill of sealing pipe joints and preventing water damage in high-pressure systems.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 01 / Isolation

Locate the primary water shut-off valve for the affected area. Turn clockwise until tight to isolate the line before beginning disassembly.

Step 02 / Drainage

Place your utility bucket beneath the joint. Open the lowest faucet in the system to bleed remaining pressure and drain residual water from the pipes.

Step 03 / Disassembly

Using your wrench, carefully unscrew the slip nut. If the fitting is stubborn, apply steady pressure—avoid jerking movements to prevent pipe warping.

Step 04 / Thread Prep

Clean the threads thoroughly. Apply 3-4 wraps of Plumber's Tape in the direction of the threads (clockwise) to ensure a watertight seal upon re-entry.

CRITICAL STAGE

Re-Engagement & Torque

Hand-tighten the fitting first to avoid cross-threading. Once snug, use the wrench for a final quarter-turn. Do not over-torque, as this can crack plastic fittings or damage metal threads.
