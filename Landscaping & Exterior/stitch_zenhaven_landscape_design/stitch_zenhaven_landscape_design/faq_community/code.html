Cultivation FAQ

How much water does moss need?

add

Moss thrives on consistency rather than volume. A fine misting twice a day—once at dawn and once at dusk—is ideal. Avoid heavy saturation, which can lead to root rot in the delicate rhizoids that anchor the moss to the ground.

Best stones for a dry garden?

add

Karesansui gardens benefit from weathered, igneous rocks like granite or basalt. Look for stones with "personality"—natural fissures, moss growth, or unique shapes that suggest a mountain peak or an island emerging from a sea of gravel.

When is the best time for pruning?

add

Pruning for structure (Niwaki) should occur in late winter while the plant is dormant. However, "candle" pruning for Japanese Black Pines is specifically done in early summer to control the vigor and needle length of new growth.

How to handle invasive weeds?

add

In a Zen garden, weeding is considered a form of meditation. Hand-pulling is preferred to maintain the integrity of the raked sand. For moss gardens, use a thin knife to lift weeds from the root without disturbing the surrounding moss carpet.
