CORE PRINCIPLES

Stone & Water Balance

The Japanese garden is a microcosm of nature. Harmony is found through the intentional placement of Ishigumi (stone) and the presence of Mizu (water), representing the eternal dialogue between strength and fluidity.

Ishigumi: The Bones

In the Ishigumi tradition, stones are seen as the backbone of the landscape. They represent stability, longevity, and the sacred mountains. Selection begins with the 'Soul Stone', which dictates the energy of the entire composition.

Mizu: The Life Blood

Water serves as the mirror for the sky. Whether through a literal pond or the symbolic raked gravel of a Karesansui (dry garden), water introduces motion and reflection, softening the rigid permanence of the stone.
