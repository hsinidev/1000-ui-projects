How to determine the optimal Mach Number for subsonic boundary layers? expand_more

For subsonic boundary layer analysis, ensure the Reynolds number (Re) is calculated based on the characteristic length (L). Mach numbers below 0.3 are typically treated as incompressible. Beyond 0.3, compressibility effects become non-negligible.

Selection criteria for k-epsilon vs. k-omega turbulence models? expand_more

The k-omega (SST) model is superior for wall-bounded flows and predicting flow separation. Use k-epsilon for initial iterations of complex industrial geometries where robustness is prioritized over near-wall precision.

Re-Independence Steady State
Mitigating divergence in high-enthalpy flow simulations? expand_more

Reduce Courant-Friedrichs-Lewy (CFL) numbers gradually. Start with a CFL of 0.5 and increment by 0.1 once residuals show a monotonic decay of at least 10^-3.
