The Temporal Condition Scale

A rigorous framework for the assessment of vintage horological artifacts. We prioritize the preservation of historical integrity over cosmetic perfection, valuing the unique narrative told through original components and natural aging.

CURRENT REVISION

OCTOBER 2024

GRADE 01

New Old Stock (NOS)

Zero visible signs of wear. The case remains factory-original with sharp, unpolished chamfers. Original factory stickers or seals may still be present.

  • check_circle Original Case Integrity
  • check_circle Zero Patina Development
GRADE 02

Excellent

Minor hairline surface marks consistent with careful wear. The movement remains unmolested, and the dial exhibits vibrant, consistent color.

GRADE 03

Very Good

Appreciable signs of honest use. Lume plots may have aged to a desirable 'creamy' or 'pumpkin' hue. Case may have seen light sympathetic polishing.

GRADE 04

Fair / Distressed

Significant evidence of a functional life. Heavy patina, "ghosted" bezels, and rounded case edges. Valued for its raw character and provenance.

THE SCRUTINY PROTOCOL

Case Integrity

We assess the remaining metal on the lugs and the presence of factory brush patterns. A 'thick' case is a primary value driver.

Dial Preservation

The dial is the soul of the watch. We look for spotting, oxidation, and the stability of tritium or radium elements.
