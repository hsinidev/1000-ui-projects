01. General

What defines the CALIBRE-X as an "In-House" movement?

expand_more

Every bridge, plate, and wheel is engineered within our micro-manufactory. Unlike modular movements, the CALIBRE-X integrates the base plate and complication framework into a single monobloc chassis, ensuring 0.002mm tolerances across all axes.

#REF-A102
CHASSIS-INTEGRITY

How does the silicon escapement improve chronometric performance?

expand_more

By utilizing Deep Reactive Ion Etching (DRIE), we create escapement wheels that are entirely anti-magnetic and require zero lubrication. This reduces friction and inertia, maintaining a steady 4Hz beat rate regardless of external gravitational forces.

02. Complications

Can the moon-phase indicator be adjusted at any time?

expand_more

No. Adjustment should be avoided between 22:00 and 02:00. During this window, the movement's mechanical brain is engaged in the date and phase jump. Manual interference during this critical alignment period can strain the gear train teeth.

warning CAUTION: MECANISM INTERFERENCE PROTOCOL

Is the GMT module integrated or modular?

expand_more

The CALIBRE-X GMT is fully integrated. The second time zone hand is driven directly by the main gear train, ensuring that tracking two time zones does not deplete the 72-hour power reserve faster than standard timekeeping.

03. Maintenance

What is the recommended interval for a full overhaul?

expand_more

Due to the high-tech lubricants and silicon components used in our atelier, we recommend a complete movement overhaul every 7 to 10 years. Water resistance seals, however, should be pressure tested annually.

How should I wind the movement after it has stopped?

expand_more

Unscrew the crown and rotate it clockwise approximately 30-40 times. You cannot "overwind" the CALIBRE-X as it features a slipping-clutch safety mechanism designed to prevent mainspring breakage.
