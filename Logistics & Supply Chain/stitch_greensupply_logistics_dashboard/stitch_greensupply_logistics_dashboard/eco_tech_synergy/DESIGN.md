## Brand & Style

The brand identity bridges the gap between high-efficiency logistics and environmental stewardship. The design system targets corporate decision-makers and sustainability officers who require precision data presented through a lens of ecological responsibility. 

The aesthetic is a hybrid of **Minimalism** and **Tactile** design. It utilizes expansive whitespace and high-quality typography to ensure clarity in complex data visualization. To evoke the "Eco-Tech" feel, the interface incorporates subtle organic textures—such as grain overlays resembling recycled paper or micro-patterns mimicking leaf structures—layered beneath sharp, digital-first UI elements. The emotional response is one of grounded innovation: professional, reliable, and deeply connected to the natural world.

## Colors

The palette is anchored by **Forest Green**, representing stability and growth. **Off-White** serves as the primary canvas color to reduce eye strain and provide a cleaner, more "breathable" atmosphere than pure white. 

**Earthy Brown** is reserved for high-level typography and structural elements to provide a grounded contrast, while **Lime Green** acts as the high-energy "Eco-Tech" accent for calls to action, success states, and data highlights. Data visualization should utilize a monochromatic green scale supplemented by the Earthy Brown for baseline metrics.

## Typography

This design system utilizes **Manrope** for its primary communication. Its geometric yet humanist qualities align perfectly with the "Eco-Tech" narrative, offering excellent legibility for technical logistics data. 

**Space Grotesk** is introduced as a secondary functional typeface for labels, data points, and technical metadata. Its slight "tech" quirkiness reinforces the consultancy’s innovative edge. Headlines should use tighter tracking and heavier weights to feel impactful, while body copy maintains generous line heights to ensure a clean, professional reading experience.

## Layout & Spacing

The layout follows a **Fixed Grid** model on desktop to maintain a premium, editorial feel, transitioning to a fluid system on smaller breakpoints. A 12-column grid is standard, with 24px gutters to allow for "breathable" data sets. 

The spacing rhythm is based on an 8px linear scale. Large-scale sections should prioritize vertical padding (80px+) to evoke the openness of natural landscapes, preventing the technical data from feeling cluttered or overwhelming.

## Elevation & Depth

Hierarchy is established through **Tonal Layers** and **Ambient Shadows**. Instead of traditional grey shadows, this design system uses soft, diffused shadows tinted with the primary Forest Green (#228B22) at extremely low opacity (4-8%). This "organic depth" makes elements feel as though they are resting on a soft surface.

Cards and containers use subtle 1px borders in a slightly darker shade of the Off-White background or the Earthy Brown to define boundaries without adding visual noise. Backdrop blurs are used sparingly on navigation overlays to maintain a sense of glass-like transparency, symbolizing corporate transparency in sustainability reporting.

## Shapes

The shape language is defined by "Organic Precision." Elements use a **Rounded** (Level 2) corner radius to avoid the clinical feel of sharp corners, mirroring the curves found in nature. 

Buttons and specialized "impact metrics" may use a semi-pill shape to draw attention. Interactive elements should feel soft to the touch, reinforcing the "approachable technology" aspect of the brand.

## Components

### Buttons
Primary buttons use a solid Forest Green fill with white text. Secondary buttons utilize an Earthy Brown outline with a subtle 5% fill on hover. Tertiary buttons are text-only with a Lime Green underline to indicate growth and movement.

### Cards
Cards are the primary container for data. They should feature a white background, a Level 2 corner radius, and a soft tinted shadow. Where possible, include a subtle grain texture in the background of the card to evoke high-quality recycled paper.

### Data Visualization
Charts should avoid sharp 90-degree angles; line graphs should use rounded joints, and bar charts should have rounded tops. Use Forest Green for primary data and Lime Green for "Target" or "Green" benchmarks.

### Input Fields & Controls
Inputs use a light grey-brown border that thickens and changes to Forest Green on focus. Checkboxes and radio buttons use the organic roundedness of the system, with the Lime Green accent signifying an "active/positive" choice.

### Supply Chain Chips
Used for tracking logistics status. Use "Leaf" shapes (rounded on two opposing corners) to represent packages or nodes in the supply chain, color-coded by their environmental impact score.
