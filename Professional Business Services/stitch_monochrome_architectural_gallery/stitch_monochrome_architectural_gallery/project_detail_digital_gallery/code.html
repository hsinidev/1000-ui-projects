STRUCTURAL CONCEPT

The Monolith House represents a radical reduction of form to its most essential geometric components. Located on a steep alpine slope, the structure is conceived as a singular volume of cast-in-place concrete that emerges directly from the bedrock.

By utilizing a 'Blueprint-to-Build' methodology, the design maintains absolute fidelity between the initial technical drafting and the final physical manifestation. Every joint, every pour line, and every glass intersection was calculated to the millimeter, ensuring that the finished product reflects the mathematical purity of its original drawing.
