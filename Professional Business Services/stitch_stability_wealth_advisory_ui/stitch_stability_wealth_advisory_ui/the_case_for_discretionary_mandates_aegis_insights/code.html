The Case for Discretionary Mandates in Volatile Markets

Marcus Thorne, CFA

Chief Investment Officer • October 24, 2024

In an era defined by rapid information flow and unprecedented global interconnectedness, market volatility has become the new structural baseline. For high-net-worth individuals and institutional stewards, the challenge is no longer just identifying opportunity, but executing decisions with the speed and precision required to preserve capital during downward shifts.

Navigating Market Turbulence

Traditional advisory models often rely on a consultative approach where every rebalancing decision requires explicit client approval. While this ensures total transparency, it introduces a critical latency in decision-making. In volatile markets, the window to act—either to hedge risk or capitalize on a temporary mispricing—can close in minutes, not days.

A discretionary mandate solves this by delegating the execution authority to the investment manager within predefined risk parameters. This allows for immediate response to macroeconomic shifts, ensuring that the strategic asset allocation remains aligned with the client’s long-term objectives without the friction of administrative delays.

"The true value of discretion is not found in the freedom to trade, but in the institutional rigor to remain disciplined when the market loses its sense of proportion."

The Fiduciary Advantage

Under a discretionary framework, the manager’s fiduciary duty is heightened. At Thorne Wealth, our approach to discretion is governed by an Investment Policy Statement (IPS) that acts as the immutable constitution of the portfolio. This ensures that even in periods of high-velocity trading, every action is anchored in the client's specific tax situation, risk tolerance, and liquidity needs.

Furthermore, discretionary management allows for sophisticated tax-loss harvesting and block trading efficiencies that are often inaccessible to individual retail accounts. By aggregating orders across multiple mandates, we can achieve superior execution prices and lower transaction costs, providing a silent but significant boost to net returns over time.
