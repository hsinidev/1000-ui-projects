The Experience

A sensory assault in the most exquisite way. This dish defines the legendary "mala" profile—a combination of searing heat and a unique tingling numbness. The aromatic intensity of toasted Szechuan peppercorns pairs with flash-seared flank steak to create a texture that is simultaneously crisp and tender. Each bite vibrates on the palate, releasing notes of citrus and woodsmoke.

Essential Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs Flank Steak Thinly sliced against the grain
  • 3 tbsp Szechuan Peppercorns Toasted and coarsely ground
  • 8 Dried Red Lantern Chilis Whole for aroma, or halved for intense heat
  • Aromatic Base Fresh ginger, garlic cloves, and scallion whites
  • Black Vinegar & Soy Chinkiang vinegar and dark soy sauce blend

CHEF'S NOTES

Understanding 'Mala': The numbing sensation (ma) from Szechuan peppercorns isn't heat—it's a physical vibration.

Safety Tip: When toasting peppercorns, keep your head back. The fumes are potent and can cause coughing if inhaled directly. Toast just until the first puff of smoke appears.

wine_bar
WINE PAIRING

The numbing heat of Szechuan peppercorns pairs beautifully with high-acid, low-alcohol wines. We recommend an off-dry Riesling to cut through the spice, or a chilled Gamay for its bright red fruit notes.

Preparation Method

01

THE INFUSION

Toast whole peppercorns in a dry wok until fragrant. Grind coarsely while still warm. This unlocks the essential oils responsible for the tingling sensation.

02

THE SEAR

Heat oil until smoking. Flash-fry the beef in small batches to ensure it browns and crisps rather than steams. Remove and set aside immediately.

03

THE HARMONY

Sauté aromatics and chilis. Return beef to the wok, toss with ground peppercorns and sauce. Glaze until sticky and intensely aromatic.
