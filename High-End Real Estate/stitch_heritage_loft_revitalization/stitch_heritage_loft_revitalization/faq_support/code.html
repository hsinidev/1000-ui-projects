Heritage Preservation

What defines a "Restoration Certified" loft within the Heritage Registry? expand_more
A Restoration Certified loft must maintain at least 70% of its original structural elements, including exposed timber, masonry, and industrial fenestration. Every unit undergoes a rigorous 12-month revitalisation process overseen by the Heritage Trust to ensure historical integrity meets modern safety standards.
Can residents make interior modifications to historical surfaces? expand_more
To preserve the soulful character of the masonry, residents are prohibited from painting or drilling into primary heritage brickwork. However, modular interior walls and specialized hanging systems are provided to allow for personalization without compromising the architectural narrative.

Leasing & Sales

What is the required investment profile for heritage property acquisition? expand_more
Acquiring a unit within Heritage Loft requires an alignment with our long-term stewardship model. Prospective buyers are interviewed by the Trust to ensure an appreciation for historic urban fabric. We prioritize residents who value community longevity over short-term speculative yields.
