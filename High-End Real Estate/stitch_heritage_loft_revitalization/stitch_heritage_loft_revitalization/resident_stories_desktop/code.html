Clara & The Loomer Building

THE CURATOR'S NOTE

Clara's unit was originally the foreman's office, overlooking the main loom floor. We preserved the original heart-pine floors which still bear the marks of 100 years of movement.

Finding Solace in Structure

When Clara first stepped into the Loomer Building, it was a skeleton of its former glory. Dust motes danced in the shafts of light cutting through broken panes, and the scent of aged cedar hung heavy in the air. Most saw a ruin; Clara saw a rhythm. As a concert violinist, she was drawn to the acoustics of the expansive, double-height ceilings and the solid, unwavering presence of the masonry walls.

The restoration was not merely about renovation, but about unveiling. Under layers of drywall, we found the original industrial windows—vast, iron-framed portals that Clara now uses as her backdrop for evening rehearsals. "The building has a voice," she says, "and I'm just here to play along with it."

format_quote

"Living here is like residing inside a piece of history that refuses to be silent. Every creak in the floorboard is a note from the past."

— Clara Vance, Resident since 2022

The integration of Clara's modern life into this historical shell is seamless. Her minimalist furniture acts as a silent observer to the architectural flourishes of the early 20th century. The kitchen, finished in charcoal steel and honed marble, sits where the building's massive steam valves once stood—a nod to the industrial power that once drove the neighborhood.
