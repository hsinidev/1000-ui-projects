Chapter IV: The Vanishing Straits

04:12 12:45

Beyond the known trade routes of the 16th century lay a phantom coastline, meticulously documented by cartographers who had never set foot on its shores. This particular map, recovered from a sealed vault in Venice, challenges our understanding of early explorations.

Notice the ink density in the northwestern quadrant. The cartographer used a rare crushed lapis pigment, suggesting this document was not intended for a common navigator, but as a prestigious gift for a royal treasury. The myths depicted—sea serpents and celestial beings—served as both navigational warnings and political metaphors for the dangers of the unknown.
