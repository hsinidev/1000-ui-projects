Frequently Asked Questions

What is a MiCA directive? expand_more
MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets) is a regulation in EU law that provides a comprehensive regulatory framework for digital assets not covered by existing financial services legislation. It aims to support innovation while ensuring consumer protection and market integrity across all EU member states.
How does the SEC determine if a token is a security? expand_more
The SEC primarily uses the 'Howey Test' which looks at whether there is an investment of money in a common enterprise with a reasonable expectation of profits to be derived from the efforts of others. If these four criteria are met, the asset is typically deemed a security.
What are the reporting requirements for FINRA registered firms? expand_more
Registered firms must comply with rigorous record-keeping, anti-money laundering (AML) protocols, and periodic reporting of digital asset activity. This includes notifying FINRA of any material changes to their business operations involving digital assets.
