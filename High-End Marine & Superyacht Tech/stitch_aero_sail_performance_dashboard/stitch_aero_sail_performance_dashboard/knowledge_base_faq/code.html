Technical Protocol: Foil-Assisted Upwind Transitions

To maximize velocity made good (VMG) during upwind legs, the foil rake must be dynamically adjusted relative to the apparent wind angle (AWA). For optimal lift-to-drag ratios, we recommend a 2.5-degree positive pitch when TWS is between 14-18 knots.

When transitioning through a tack, the 'Flight Controller' must initiate hydraulic release 2.4 seconds prior to the helm movement to prevent structural loading on the main bearing plates.

CAUTION: EXCEEDING 4.0 DEGREES OF RAKE IN SEAS EXCEEDING 1.5M MAY RESULT IN CAVITATION AND SUBSEQUENT LOSS OF CONTROL.

Documentation of the last hull inspection (Log 4402) confirms that the foil-well seals have been reinforced for these specific loads.
