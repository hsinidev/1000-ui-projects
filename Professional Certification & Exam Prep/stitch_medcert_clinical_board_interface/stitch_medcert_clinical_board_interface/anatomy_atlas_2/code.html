ANATOMICAL OVERVIEW

bolt

The sternum is a flat bone located in the center of the chest. It consists of three parts: the manubrium, the body, and the xiphoid process. It serves as a key attachment point for the clavicles and first seven pairs of ribs.

medical_services

CLINICAL CORRELATION

Sternal Puncture

Because the sternum is subcutaneous and contains highly vascular hematopoietic marrow, it is a common site for bone marrow aspiration in adults. The procedure is typically performed in the upper half of the manubrium.

lightbulb KEY FINDINGS (USMLE)
  • • The "Angle of Louis" (Sternal Angle) corresponds to the level of the T4/T5 intervertebral disc.
  • • Second rib articulates at the sternal angle.
  • • Tracheal bifurcation occurs at the same horizontal plane.
