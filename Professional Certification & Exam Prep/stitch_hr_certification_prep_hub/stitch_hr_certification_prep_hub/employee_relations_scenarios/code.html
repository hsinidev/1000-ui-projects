The Performance Pivot

Marcus, a senior analyst with six years of tenure, has shown a significant decline in output over the last two quarters. His manager, Sarah, wants to initiate a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) immediately. However, Marcus recently disclosed he is struggling with a chronic health condition that requires intermittent leave.

The Situation:

Sarah is frustrated and feels Marcus is using his condition as a shield against accountability. She approaches you, the HR Business Partner, demanding the PIP move forward today to "protect the department's metrics."

report_problem

Compliance Risk Quiz

What is the most legally sound and empathetic HR response to Sarah's request?

Option A

Approve the PIP but include a disclaimer about FMLA rights. Sarah's concern for department metrics is valid, and performance standards must be maintained regardless of personal circumstances.

Option B (Selected)

Pause the PIP and initiate an ADA interactive process. We must determine if the performance decline is linked to the medical condition and explore reasonable accommodations before taking disciplinary action.

check_circle

Correct Approach

Initiating a PIP immediately after a medical disclosure risks a retaliation claim. The ADA "interactive process" is the mandated first step to ensure fair treatment and legal compliance.
