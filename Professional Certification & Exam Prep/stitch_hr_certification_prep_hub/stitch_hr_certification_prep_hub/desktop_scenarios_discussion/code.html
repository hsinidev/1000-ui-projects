The Performance Pivot

Navigating a sensitive termination risk involving long-term performance decline and a sudden FMLA leave request.

description

Narrative Context

Alex, a Senior Marketing Manager with 8 years of tenure, has shown a significant decline in output quality over the last 12 months. After multiple informal coaching sessions, management initiated a formal Performance Improvement Plan (PIP).

During the 30-day review meeting, where termination was identified as the next likely step due to lack of progress, Alex immediately informed the manager that they had been struggling with a chronic health condition and intended to file for FMLA leave starting the next day.

"The timing is suspect, but the medical documentation provided 48 hours later appears legitimate and signed by a specialist. We need to determine the risk of proceeding with the PIP vs. approving the leave."

Key Evidence:

  • documented history of coaching (Jan - March)
  • PIP signed and dated by both parties (April 1st)
  • FMLA request date: May 1st (Meeting Date)

Legal Insight

While performance documentation helps, FMLA timing creates a "rebuttable presumption" of retaliation. Always consult legal before terminating within 30 days of a leave request.
