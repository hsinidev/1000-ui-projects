Overview of California Real Estate Ethics

In accordance with Section 10176 of the Business and Professions Code, California real estate licensees are held to the highest standard of professional conduct. This module outlines the fiduciary duties owed to principals and the disclosure requirements for material facts affecting property value.

Key legal precedents included in this summary:

  • Easton v. Strassburger (1984) - Duty to conduct a reasonably competent and diligent inspection.
  • Agency Disclosure Law updates effective January 2024.
  • Handling of trust funds and anti-commingling regulations.

Failure to adhere to these standards may result in license suspension, heavy fines, or permanent revocation by the Department of Real Estate (DRE).
