Scaffolding vs. Direct Instruction in Large Groups?

I'm struggling with implementing effective scaffolding techniques in my classroom of 35 students. It feels like direct instruction is the only way to keep the pace. Has anyone successfully used zone of proximal development strategies in high-density environments without losing classroom control?

#vvgotsky #classroom_management
Dr. Elena Rodriguez verified EXPERT VERIFIED
1 hour ago

Marcus, this is a common challenge! One tactile strategy is to use "Instructional Placemats." These are pre-designed templates that provide visual scaffolding cues for groups. This allows you to facilitate 6 groups of 6 students effectively, moving between them while they support each other using the provided scaffolds.

lightbulb
STUDY TIP

Remember, scaffolding is temporary. The goal is to gradually remove the "placemats" as students gain competency.
