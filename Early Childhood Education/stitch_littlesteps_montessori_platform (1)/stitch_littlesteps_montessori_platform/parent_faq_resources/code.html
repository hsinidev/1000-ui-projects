Montessori Philosophy

What makes the Montessori approach unique? expand_more

The Montessori method is based on self-directed activity, hands-on learning, and collaborative play. In our classrooms, children make creative choices in their learning, while the classroom and the highly trained teacher offer age-appropriate activities to lead the process. Every material is designed to be self-correcting and tactile, encouraging independence and a lifelong love for learning.

How do multi-age classrooms benefit my child? expand_more

Multi-age groupings (typically 3-year spans) allow older children to become mentors and leaders, reinforcing their own knowledge as they help others. Younger children feel supported by their peers and are naturally inspired by the more advanced work they see happening around them. This mimics real-world social structures and fosters a strong sense of community.

Daily Routines

What does a typical day look like? expand_more

Our day is centered around the "Work Cycle," an uninterrupted period where children choose their activities.

  • 08:30 — Arrival and Greeting
  • 09:00 — Morning Work Cycle (Focus and Exploration)
  • 11:30 — Community Circle and Song
  • 12:00 — Shared Lunch and Outdoor Play
How do you handle meals and snacks? expand_more

Snack time is an exercise in "Practical Life." Children are encouraged to prepare their own snacks (with supervision), set their places, and clean up afterward. We prioritize organic, whole foods and foster an environment of gratitude and social grace during shared meals.
