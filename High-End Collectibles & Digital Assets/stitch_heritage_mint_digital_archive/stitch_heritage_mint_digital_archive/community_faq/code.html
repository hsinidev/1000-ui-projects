MANUSCRIPT #882

Observations on the 14th-century Petrarch Folio

Curator Elena V. • 2 hours ago

The marginalia found on page 14 of the Petrarch Folio suggests a previously undocumented owner from the Avignon period. The specific ink composition seems to match the reagents used by the Papal Chancellery during that era. Has anyone else noticed the floral watermark under UV light?

Archivist Julian 45 mins ago

I cross-referenced the watermark with the Briquet archive. It matches a guild in the Rhone valley, active between 1350 and 1375. This essentially confirms the Avignon provenance.

Curator Elena V. 10 mins ago

Remarkable. Julian, could you upload the Briquet comparison scan to the Provenance Vault for this manuscript? It would significantly boost the rarity score.

HOW IS PROVENANCE VERIFIED? expand_more
Each manuscript undergoes a multi-layer verification process including physical chemical analysis, historical record cross-referencing, and a consensus vote by the Archivist Board before being minted.
TOKENIZATION STANDARDS expand_more
We use ERC-721H (Heritage) standards which include on-chain storage for metadata regarding physical degradation and ownership history, ensuring the digital twin evolves with the artifact.
PHYSICAL VAULT SAFETY expand_more
Manuscripts are stored in climate-controlled, nitrogen-purged chambers in a high-security facility located in the Swiss Alps, monitored 24/7 by both biometrics and decentralized oracles.
